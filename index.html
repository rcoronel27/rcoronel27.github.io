Personal Statement

  As an information science major, I want to use my skills in data analysis to create good in the world. I started as a computer science major, but I felt unfulfilled by seemingly vacuous coding. My decision to switch from computer science to information science wasn’t easy, but it was necessary. I realized I was more interested in the impact of data than the abstract mechanics behind it. Information science offered the bridge I needed between technology and people. I hope to continue learning the ways how information affects people and how to convey it in accessible ways to users and my future coworkers.
  In addition, as an Asian American studies minor, I know full well the need to preserve Asian American culture and history in our ever-changing society, and I am a firm advocate for diversity in any team, workplace, or situation I find myself in. Both my coursework in Asian American studies and my involvement in the Filipino Cultural Association inform how I approach data ethics and representation, ensuring that historically underrepresented people remain seen and respected.
  As an incoming vice president of Ganji, UMD’s K-Pop dance club, I prioritize clear communication and adherence to group schedules to keep club operations running seamlessly. I’ve learned how to organize the logistics to support my fellow board members’ creative visions, and I’ve spent countless hours learning choreographies and tirelessly fixing details to make our performances shine.
  In the future, I hope to work at the intersection of technology and public service, helping institutions better understand and serve diverse communities through curated data use. I believe that well-rounded thinking is essential to create the tools needed to keep our world running, not just smoothly, but fairly and equitably.
